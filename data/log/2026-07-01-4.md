TapeDeck is a new one: a small macOS app that records system audio straight to
MP3. Point it at BlackHole 2ch (a virtual audio driver) routed through a
Multi-Output Device, hit record, and it captures whatever's playing and drops an
MP3 when you stop.

It's plain Swift and SwiftUI, no dependencies beyond `ffmpeg` for the MP3 encode
since `afconvert` dropped MP3 support. The one wrinkle worth noting is that the
app has to be launched as a real `.app` bundle rather than a bare `swift run`
process — otherwise macOS attributes the microphone permission prompt to the
terminal instead of TapeDeck, and the app never gets asked for audio access.
`run.sh` handles the build-and-wrap step so that just works.

This is a first commit, so it's still rough — a single view for device
selection, record/stop, and a reveal-in-Finder button. No packaging or
distribution yet, just a tool for grabbing system audio without needing a full
DAW open.
